Diversity of Australia's Population

Australia is one of the most culturally diverse countries in the world. Its population of over 26 million includes people from more than 200 countries, speaking hundreds of languages and practicing many different religions. This diversity is reflected in Australia's food, traditions, and way of life.

From the First Nations peoples to migrants who have made Australia their home, each group contributes to a vibrant, inclusive society that values respect, equality, and a shared sense of belonging.

Where did we come from?

This chart shows the top 20 countries of birth for people living in Australia, excluding those born in Australia itself. The three largest groups come from England, India, and New Zealand, reflecting both historical ties and modern migration trends. Over time, there has been a clear shift, with more people from Asian countries choosing to make Australia their home, highlighting the country's growing connections with the Asia-Pacific region. The significant number of people from India also reflects Australia's evolving migration patterns and cultural links, showing how historical influences and modern opportunities continue to shape Australia's diverse population.

Population Over Time

There is a steady increase in both Australia-born and overseas-born populations over time. It tells the story of a country that continues to grow not only through migration but also through local birth rates. The upward trend suggests that many people who migrate to Australia choose to settle permanently, build their lives, and raise families here. This highlights Australia's strong appeal as a place to live and the sense of stability and opportunity it offers to both newcomers and long-term residents.

Where did we go?

The map highlights the number of overseas migrants across Australian states, adjusted to account for each state's population size. This normalisation allows for a fair comparison by showing the proportion of migrants rather than just the raw numbers. From the visualisation, it is evident that Victoria, New South Wales, and Western Australia stand out as the states with the highest levels of overseas migration relative to their populations.

This pattern likely reflects the strong economic growth, diverse job markets, and world-class education systems in these regions, which attract international students and skilled workers alike. Additionally, these states are home to major urban centres such as Melbourne, Sydney, and Perth, offering vibrant lifestyles and cultural diversity that make them appealing destinations for migrants seeking new opportunities in Australia.

From Arrival to Citizenship

For most years, the number of people arriving in Australia on temporary visas—such as student, working holiday, and business visas—has been significantly higher than the number of arrivals with permanent residency or Australian citizenship. However, in 2020, this trend reversed. Due to COVID-19 travel restrictions and border closures, the number of Australian citizens returning home exceeded the number of temporary visa holders entering the country, as international travel and visa programs were largely suspended.

Many people from overseas choose to become Australian citizens for various benefits, such as greater stability, rights, and opportunities. In particular, citizens from New Zealand often make up a large portion of new citizenships, as the process is generally easier for them compared to other nationalities. Additionally, the greatest number of people in the “Other” category highlights Australia's increasing cultural diversity, reflecting the wide range of nationalities choosing to call Australia home.
